GEMSBOK

The gemsbok, gemsbuck or South African oryx (Oryx gazella) is a large antelope in the genus Oryx. It is native to the arid regions of Southern Africa, such as the Kalahari Desert. Some authorities formerly included the East African oryx as a subspecies. The gemsbok is depicted on the coat of arms of Namibia,[3] where the current population of the species is estimated at 373,000 individuals.

Gemsbok is a herbivore.

WHERE WILL YOU FIND THEM

In South Africa, find gemsbok in many reserves and parks, including... •Mokala National Park •Kruger National Park •Langjan Nature Reserve •Willem Pretorius Game Reserve •Camdeboo National Park •Soetdoring Dam Nature Reserve •Mapungubwe National Park •Rolfontein Nature Reserve
